RELATED PARTY PROBLEM
FCA FCG Nov 2024 · MLR 2017 · JMLSG

The Related Party Problem

When a limited company holds a bank account, the bank's CDD obligation extends beyond the company itself to its directors, UBOs and related entities. The problem: the bank is not automatically notified when these change — creating a compliance exposure that can persist for months or years undetected.

STEP 01 — ONBOARDING

Company onboarded — CDD passes

Company A applies for a business account. The bank performs CDD under MLR 2017 reg.28: the company's directors, persons of significant control (PSCs), and UBOs are all identified, verified and screened against PEP lists and sanctions databases. All pass. The account is opened.

STEP 02 — POST-ONBOARDING

Ownership chain exists — bank sees only Company A

Unknown to the bank at the time, Company A is 51% owned by Company B, which is in turn 51% owned by Company C. Company B and C have their own directors and related persons. These individuals are related parties of the bank's customer — but they are not customers of the bank themselves.

STEP 03 — THE EVENT

Director change filed at Companies House

A new director is appointed to Company A and the change is filed at Companies House. The company is legally obliged to make this disclosure — but the bank is not automatically notified. Unless the bank monitors Companies House filings in real time, this change is invisible. The bank's account T&Cs may require notification, but customer compliance with this obligation is rarely enforced.

STEP 04 — THE EXPOSURE

New director fails screening — bank doesn't know

The new director is a PEP, a sanctioned individual, or has significant adverse media. Under MLR 2017 reg.33, this would trigger Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) — but because the bank hasn't detected the change, no EDD review is conducted. Transactions continue flowing through the account. The bank is potentially non-compliant with reg.28(11) (ongoing monitoring) and the FCA FCG's connected parties obligation.

REGULATORY OBLIGATIONS IN PLAY

MLR 2017 reg.28(11)

Ongoing monitoring: banks must keep CDD documents up to date, including the ownership and control structure of legal entities. Changes must be detected and reflected.

MLR 2017 reg.28(3)

Banks must identify beneficial owners and understand the ownership and control structure of any legal person they do business with — not just the named entity.

FCA FCG (Nov 2024)

Firms must identify connected parties and corporate structures that may be subject to sanctions — not just the named customer. The Nov 2024 update made this explicit.

MLR 2017 reg.33

EDD is required where a related party is a PEP, connected to a high-risk third country, or shows adverse media. Failure to detect the trigger means EDD is never applied.

THE COMPLIANCE STANDARD — NOT OMNISCIENCE, BUT DOCUMENTED REASONABLE MEASURES

The FCA does not expect banks to know about every director change the instant it happens. The standard is risk-based reasonable measures. A bank with no process for detecting director changes at all is clearly non-compliant. A bank with a documented, risk-proportionate monitoring system (e.g. Companies House API monitoring for high-risk customers, annual refresh for standard-risk) that a change slips through is in a much stronger position. The FCA's 2025 multi-firm CDD review found that inadequate event-driven review triggers were a systemic weakness across UK banks — most policies relied on RM judgement rather than automated systematic detection.

Architecture principles
RAG — No Hallucination
All LLM outputs are grounded in retrieved documents only. The narrative agent cites sources and cannot invent sanctions or decisions.
Human in the Loop
AI prepares and recommends. Humans decide and sign. FCA requires named, written EDD approval — AI cannot replace this.
Three Lines of Defence
1LoD: RM + KYC Analyst. 2LoD: Compliance Officer. 3LoD: Internal Audit. AI augments all three but does not replace them.
Documented Rationale
Every decision generates a compliance narrative meeting the FCA's #1 finding: lack of documented rationale for EDD decisions.
Event-Driven Architecture
Companies House webhooks replace periodic manual checks. Director changes trigger immediate pipeline execution — closing the related party gap.
Audit Trail by Design
Every agent output includes data sources, timestamps, model version, and retrieval log. Audit-ready for FCA inspection at any time.
